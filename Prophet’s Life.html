The Life of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

Birth and Early Life

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was born in the city of Makkah in 570 CE, into the respected tribe of Quraysh. He was born an orphan and was cared for by his grandfather and later by his uncle Abu Talib.

Youth and Reputation

As he grew up, Muhammad ﷺ became known for his honesty and trustworthiness, earning the titles "Al-Amin" (The Trustworthy). He worked as a merchant and was admired for his integrity.

Marriage to Khadijah رضي الله عنها

At age 25, he married Khadijah, a noble and wealthy businesswoman. Their marriage was full of love and respect, and Khadijah was the first to believe in his Prophethood.

First Revelation

At age 40, while meditating in the Cave of Hira, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ received the first revelation through Angel Jibreel عليه السلام. It marked the beginning of his mission.

Early Islam

He began spreading the message of Islam, facing resistance from Quraysh leaders. Despite persecution, a small group of early Muslims formed around him.

Migration to Madinah

In 622 CE, he migrated to Madinah due to increasing hostility in Makkah. This event (Hijrah) marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar.

Establishing a Community

In Madinah, the Prophet ﷺ established a strong Muslim community based on justice, brotherhood, and worship. The Constitution of Madinah united Muslims and non-Muslims in peaceful coexistence.

Major Battles

He led Muslims in battles such as Badr, Uhud, and the Trench. Despite hardships, he maintained patience, humility, and faith in Allah.

Conquest of Makkah

In 630 CE, the Prophet ﷺ peacefully conquered Makkah, forgiving his enemies and removing idols from the Kaaba. His mercy amazed even his former opponents.

Farewell Pilgrimage

He delivered his final sermon at Mount Arafat, emphasizing equality, justice, and the rights of others. He reminded Muslims to follow the Qur'an and Sunnah.

Passing and Legacy

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ passed away in 632 CE. His legacy lives on in the hearts of over a billion Muslims worldwide, who continue to follow his example of mercy, truth, and guidance.
