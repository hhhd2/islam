Prophets in Islam

Peace and blessings upon all the prophets (عليهم السلام)

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

The final messenger of Islam, sent as a mercy to all worlds. Born in Mecca in 570 CE, he received the Qur’an through Jibreel (عليه السلام). His life is a model of mercy, wisdom, and justice.

Prophet Adam (عليه السلام)

The first human and prophet, created by Allah from clay. He lived in Paradise and was taught the names of all things, teaching humanity the value of knowledge and repentance.

Prophet Nuh (عليه السلام)

Prophet Nuh (عليه السلام) preached monotheism for centuries. He built an ark by Allah's command before the great flood. His story reflects patience and unwavering faith.

Prophet Ibrahim (عليه السلام)

Known as the friend of Allah (Khalilullah), he challenged idol worship and called people to Tawheed. He and his son Ismail (عليه السلام) raised the foundations of the Kaaba.

Prophet Musa (عليه السلام)

Sent to free the Israelites from Pharaoh. Allah granted him miracles like the parting of the Red Sea. He received the Torah and was known for his courage and direct speech with Allah.

Prophet Isa (عليه السلام)

Prophet Isa (عليه السلام) was born miraculously to Maryam (عليها السلام) and performed many miracles by Allah’s permission. He preached love, mercy, and monotheism and was raised by Allah.

Prophet Yusuf (عليه السلام)

Known for his beauty, wisdom, and patience. Interpreted dreams and rose from a well to a palace. His story teaches forgiveness and trust in Allah's plan.

Prophet Sulaiman (عليه السلام)

Son of Dawud (عليه السلام), he ruled with unmatched wisdom. He could speak to animals and command jinn. He remained humble and grateful to Allah despite his vast kingdom.
